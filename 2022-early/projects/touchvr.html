I created a virtual reality experience where the visuals inside the VR headset match the real world environment. I built a standalone door that is tracked and displayed in VR, so the user can open and close the door, and see its movement through the headset. I used Vive trackers to also track other objects.


The experience, which ran through a Vive Pro, also simulated the users' hands. By using a Leap Motion tracker, I was able to let users see a representation of their hands in VR. I taped a custom 3D printed slot to hold the Leap Motion on the headset.




This project was part of a research experiment that I conducted. I researched how we perceive time under VR influence, and I presented my study in IEEE VR 2022.


My conference paper.

My work was featured on the UNCG Research Magazine.


Developed on Unity Engine using C#.
