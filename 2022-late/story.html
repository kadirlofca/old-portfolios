STORY

WHY DO I DO WHAT I DO?


MOTIVATION

Spiderman 2 (2004) on my father's computer got me into gaming. I loved everything about this game, but I never managed to beat it as a kid. Later in life, I found the same game disc and finally beat the game. This was around the time Lego Star Wars came out. As a Lego aficionado who spent his childhood obsessed with Lego, I got my hands on the game and played it over and over again on my Playstation 2. I started playing mostly Lego games, and didn't even bother trying other ones. That was until one incredible game came out...

Minecraft was the perfect game for someone who played Lego all their life; a game with endless creative freedom. I quickly fell in love with the game and played it for years. I built amazing worlds and made great friendships. One day, one of my Minecraft friends invited me to play another game, Team Fortress 2. This is where I experienced a video game revolution. I got introduced to Steam as I was trying to figure out where to download Team Fortress 2 from. Hundreds of incredible games, right in front of me...

As I discovered and played different genres from Steam, a desire to make my own game grew inside me.

WORK

My first attempt at making a game was by downloading a 2D game engine. My dreams were quickly shattered not only because the engine was complicated and confusing, but also because I had no idea what programming was. I remember vividly how lost I was trying to grasp the concept of a scene/level. I stopped trying and started looking elsewhere to make my game.

I came across a website called Sploder. This website let you drag and drop game elements to make your own Flash game. I instantly made an account and made a game. Although I was happy and proud of my progress, Sploder wasn't enough for my ambitious plans. I needed more freedom to make my specific games. So I continued my journey to figure out how to make games.

This is where I meet Unity. I was super excited to learn Unity, I started trying out different features from the UI, and I pretty much figured out nothing. The problem was, once again, programming. I had no clue what programming was and how it worked. So I uninstalled Unity. I was frustrated, so many ideas with no way of bringing them to life. I let go and forgot about game development...

Although I stopped trying to make games, I kept playing them and I still had an itch to make games. One day I decided to learn coding. I followed a YouTube tutorial to make a calculator in Python. I successfully made the calculator. I was starting to understand how code worked, and I found it fascinating. Like Lego, like Minecraft, coding was a tool for me to create awesome stuff.

I grew confident in coding and decided to try out Unity again. I mistakenly downloaded Unreal Engine 4 instead. I did not realize my mistake at the time, because all game engines looked the same to me.
