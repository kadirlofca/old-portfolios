JUL 2022

1 SLICE & DICE GAME

JUL 2022

Game jam submission

Brief: Game I worked on for GMTK 2022. I teamed up with a friend to complete this project within the 48 hour limit.

My Role Programmer Game-Designer Artist

Tools C# Unity Plastic-SCM Blender Affinity-Designer Audacity

Design Theme of the jam was "Roll of the dice." I designed a game where a physics-based dice would be thrown at enemies to damage them. The game consists of a single level that starts small and ends in a large boss fight.

Aim Assist Player controls a character from a top down view. This character can pick up and throw a dice to damage the enemies. I programmed an aim assist mechanic that locks onto the closes enemy. This reduced the skill requirement and put more emphasis on the roll of the dice.

Coroutines Enemies can fight back by picking up the dice thrown by the player. I used a coroutine to make an interval in which the enemy would wait before trying to throw the dice.

Rigidbody By attaching rigidbodies to the character and enemies I was able to easily convert input into movement. This made dealing with collisions much easier too.

Art I created the character art using Affinity Designer, modeled and textured the dice and level using Blender. I animated the characters by stretching and squashing them in the y-axis.

MAGNOLIA VR TOUR
FEB 2021 - AUG 2021

2 MAGNOLIA VR TOUR

FEB 2021 - AUG 2021

VR Software Development

Brief: I developed a VR tour of the historic Magnolia House. I worked with an interior architecture student who did the modeling.

My Role Programmer UX-Designer

Tools C# Unity GitHub Blender SketchUp Audacity

Why My first professional software development experience. I worked for a client to create this incredible VR experience.

Navigation The user is able to explore both floors of the house by teleporting. I programmed a navmesh that appears when the user is targeting the teleportation ray.

Lighting I used Unity's lighting and reflection tools to create believable lighting. I positioned lights outside the house next to the windows to simulate sunlight coming in.
The glasses and mirrors utilize the reflection probes I positioned inside each room.

Shaders I created the materials and shaders myself using the shader graph tool. Without imperfections, the environment didn't look believable, so I added noise textures to add detail on surfaces.

Optimization I optimized the performance by utilizing frustum and occlusion culling inside Unity. But that wasn't enough. The model which was provided by my team wasn't optimized for a game engine. I used Blender to delete vertices that weren't going to be seen by the user.

3 VR RESEARCH

AUG 2021 - JAN 2023

Undergraduate research

Brief: Research I did to find why time is perceived differently in VR.
I worked on it for over a year with a team of 4.

My Role Programmer Lead-Researcher

Tools C# Unity GitLab Blender R RStudio SteamVR OpenXR

Simulation For this to be a controlled experiment, I had to simulate a VR equivalent of the room the experiment was conducted in. I use Blender to model the room and Affinity Designer to create textures. I used SteamVR, HTC Vive Trackers, and Leap Motion to track real objects and display them in VR. This created a virtual environment that perfectly matched the real world. I used Unity to simulate the room.

Automation In order to record hundreds of data points into our database, I programmed a tool for Unity that automates the process. After I finished collecting data with the help of this tool, I used R and RStudio for data analysis.

Design I designed an experiment that would compare the user's perception of time in VR with physical props and VR with virtual props. This experiment showed us if physicality in VR has an effect on time perception.

Results Alongside broadening my programming and technical skills, I learned how to do research and critical, unbiased thinking. I put out two research papers and was featured in some articles and a science magazine.

HackNC Workshop Instructor

Brief: I prepared and instructed a game-dev workshop during Hack NC 2022. Another volunteer joined me later on.

Tools Godot Visual-Scripting GitHub

Why Every year, UNC Chapel Hill hosts the HackNC hackathon. Various workshops are held during the event. I was invited to prepare and instruct a game development workshop during HackNC 2022.

Game I made a game to demonstrate game development fundamentals. The player rotates an ever-speeding spaceship to avoid colliding with planets. A timer is displayed on the screen and their goal is to last long enough and beat their highscore.

Visual Scripting I used Godot's visual scripting tool for the entire project so hackers who are new to coding can also join the workshop.

Team I started working on the game and slides solo, but was later joined by another volunteer who helped me. I communicated with other HackNC volunteers and organizers regularly in meetings.
